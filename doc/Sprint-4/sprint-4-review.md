## Process Reflection

### Decisions That Turned Out Well

#### Effective Use of CI/CD for Test Reliability
Our adoption of CI/CD pipelines proved highly beneficial, particularly in maintaining test stability during merges. This approach ensured that only passing builds were integrated, leading to a more stable codebase.

#### Accurate Capacity Estimation
Our team's estimation of workload capacity for this iteration was well-calibrated. This allowed us to plan realistically and complete the majority of our objectives without overcommitting or experiencing burnout.

---

### Decisions That Did Not Turn Out as Well as Expected

#### Avoidance of a Single Branch Structure
We decided against using a single-branch model, anticipating it might negatively impact Git history clarity. However, this led to occasional confusion and merge conflicts across multiple branches. In retrospect, a more unified branching strategy, paired with proper version control practices, could have streamlined our workflow.

---

### Planned Changes
There are currently no planned changes to our team processes for the upcoming iteration.

## Meeting Highlights and Key Takeaways

- **Maintain Effective Use of CI/CD**  
  Our CI/CD setup contributed significantly to build stability and should remain a core part of our development workflow.

- **Continue Estimating Workload Conservatively**  
  Our accurate capacity planning allowed for efficient workload distribution without overburdening team members.

- **Re-evaluate Branching Strategy**  
  The multi-branch approach caused some confusion and merge conflicts; we should explore adopting a more streamlined branching model in future iterations.

- **Prioritize Core Features Over Extras**  
  Our decision to focus on critical deliverables like scheduling, data visualization, and UI redesign proved effective. Optional features should be scheduled only if capacity allows.
